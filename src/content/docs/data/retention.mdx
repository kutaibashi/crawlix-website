## Why Set a Retention Policy?

Every crawl session is stored locally. If you run daily scheduled crawls, the database grows continuously. Without a retention policy, this can consume significant disk space over time.

Configuring a retention policy automates cleanup so you don't have to manage it manually.

## Configuring Retention

Go to **Settings > Data Management > Retention**.

## Policy Options

### Delete Sessions Older Than N Days

Automatically remove crawl sessions that are older than a configured number of days.

Examples:

- 30 days — keep about one month of history
- 90 days — keep a quarter of history
- 365 days — keep one year of history

### Keep Last N Sessions

Automatically remove older sessions when the total count exceeds a limit.

Examples:

- Keep last 10 sessions — always have the 10 most recent crawls
- Keep last 52 sessions — keep one year of weekly crawls

### No Automatic Cleanup

Keep all sessions indefinitely. You manage cleanup manually.

## Manual Cleanup

Delete specific sessions from the **History** view:

1. Right-click any session.
2. Click **Delete Session**.
3. Confirm deletion.

Deleted sessions cannot be recovered — export a `.crawlix` archive first if you want to keep the data.

## Checking Disk Usage

**Settings > Data Management** shows total database size and a breakdown by session. Use this to identify large sessions (e.g., a full crawl of a very large site) that are consuming disproportionate space.

## Recommended Policy

If you run scheduled crawls, use "Keep last N sessions":

- Weekly crawls: keep last 52 sessions (one year)
- Daily crawls: keep last 30 sessions (one month)

Adjust based on how much historical comparison you need and your available disk space.
