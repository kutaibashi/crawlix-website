## Overview

Customize the color scheme of your exported reports to match your agency's brand identity.

**Requires Agency tier.**

## Setting Colors

Go to **Settings > White Label > Colors**.

## Color Fields

### Primary Color

Used for main headings (H1, H2), key metric values, and the report's dominant visual identity.

Example: your agency's main brand color.

### Secondary Color

Used for section backgrounds, dividers, and supporting visual elements.

Example: a lighter shade of your primary color, or a complementary color.

### Accent Color

Used for highlights, charts, data callouts, and special elements.

Example: a contrasting color that draws attention to important figures.

## Color Format

All colors must be valid hex values: `#rrggbb` format (e.g., `#1a56db`, `#10b981`, `#f59e0b`).

## Previewing Colors

Click **Preview** to generate a sample report page using your color settings before exporting a real report. Adjust until you're satisfied.

## Contrast Considerations

Choose colors that maintain sufficient contrast for readability:

- Text on colored backgrounds should have a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1
- Dark backgrounds with light text or light backgrounds with dark text both work well
- Avoid low-contrast combinations (e.g., light gray text on white background)

## Resetting Colors

Click **Reset to Defaults** to clear your custom colors and return to the Crawlix defaults.
