## Word Count

Crawlix counts the words in the visible text content of each page (excluding navigation, footer, and other boilerplate when detected).

- **Thin content** — pages below a configurable word count threshold are flagged. The default threshold is 300 words. You can adjust this in Settings > SEO Checks > Content.
- Pages with extremely low word counts (under 50 words) are flagged as Errors; pages below the threshold but above the minimum are flagged as Warnings.

## Readability

Crawlix calculates a **Flesch-Kincaid readability score** for each page's body text.

- Scores range from 0–100. Higher scores mean easier to read.
- A score of 60–70 is considered readable for a general audience.
- Very low scores (below 30) flag the content as complex or hard to read.

Readability scoring supports both **English** and **Arabic** text.

## Keyword Density

Crawlix detects potential **keyword stuffing** by analyzing the frequency of words relative to total content. Pages with abnormally high repetition of a single word or phrase are flagged.

This is a heuristic check — it flags candidates for review, not definitive violations.

## Content-to-HTML Ratio

A low ratio of text content to total HTML indicates a page heavy with markup and light on content. Pages with very low content-to-HTML ratios are flagged.

## Near-Duplicate Content

Using **cosine similarity**, Crawlix compares the text content of pages on the same site. Pages that are very similar to other pages on the site (but not identical) are flagged as near-duplicate content.

This is useful for detecting:

- Product pages with nearly identical descriptions
- Blog posts republished with minor edits
- Category/tag pages with overlapping content

## Boilerplate Detection

Pages that consist mostly of navigation, headers, footers, and very little unique text content are flagged as boilerplate-heavy. These pages offer little unique value to search engines.

## Placeholder Content

Crawlix detects Lorem Ipsum text and other placeholder content patterns. A live page containing placeholder text is always an error.
