## Overview

Connect your Google Analytics 4 property to add traffic data directly to Crawlix's Pages view. This combines SEO audit data with actual user metrics in one place.

**Requires Pro or Agency tier.**

## Connecting GA4

1. Go to **Settings > Integrations > Google Analytics 4**.
2. Click **Connect with Google**.
3. A browser window opens for Google OAuth — sign in with your Google account.
4. Grant Crawlix access to your Analytics data.
5. Select the GA4 property you want to connect.
6. Click **Save**.

No API key is needed — the OAuth flow handles authentication securely.

## Data Synced

After connecting, Crawlix syncs the following metrics per page from your GA4 property:

- **Sessions** — number of user sessions for each URL
- **Pageviews** — total page views
- **Engagement rate** — percentage of sessions where users engaged
- **Bounce rate** — percentage of sessions with no meaningful engagement
- **Traffic sources** — organic search, direct, referral, social breakdown

Data is synced from the last 30 days by default. You can adjust the date range in integration settings.

## Viewing GA Data in Crawlix

GA4 data appears as extra columns in the **Pages** view. Sort by sessions or pageviews to find your highest-traffic pages and check their SEO health.

## Orphan Pages Analysis

One valuable use of GA4 integration: finding pages that receive traffic but have **no inbound internal links**. These are orphan pages — users can reach them (via bookmarks, external links, or direct search), but the site's internal link structure doesn't support them.

See [Orphan Pages Analysis](/docs/integrations/orphan-pages) for details.

## Data Privacy

GA4 data is fetched from Google's API directly to your machine. No GA data passes through Crawlix servers. You must have at least **Viewer** role on the GA4 property.
