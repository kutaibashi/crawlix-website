## Overview

Connect Google Search Console to bring search performance data into Crawlix. See impressions, clicks, CTR, and average position alongside your technical SEO audit data.

**Requires Pro or Agency tier.**

## Connecting Search Console

1. Go to **Settings > Integrations > Google Search Console**.
2. Click **Connect with Google**.
3. Complete the OAuth flow in the browser window.
4. Select your Search Console property from the list.
5. Click **Save**.

## Data Synced

After connecting, Crawlix syncs the following per page:

- **Impressions** — how many times the page appeared in Google search results
- **Clicks** — how many times users clicked through to the page
- **CTR** — click-through rate (clicks ÷ impressions)
- **Average position** — average rank in search results
- **Top queries** — the search terms driving the most impressions to each page

## Index Status

Crawlix also syncs coverage data:

- **Indexed** — Google has indexed the page
- **Excluded** — Google has not indexed the page, with reason (noindex, crawl anomaly, etc.)

## Viewing GSC Data in Crawlix

GSC data appears as columns in the Pages view. You can:

- Sort by impressions to find your highest-visibility pages
- Sort by average position to find pages ranking just outside the top 10 (positions 11–20 are prime for improvement)
- Filter by index status to find pages that Google isn't indexing

## GSC + Crawl Data Together

Combining Search Console data with your crawl audit reveals opportunities like:

- High-impression pages with technical SEO issues (fix issues to improve CTR)
- Well-linked pages that Google isn't indexing (investigate why)
- Pages with high clicks but poor Core Web Vitals (improve performance)

## Requirements

Your Google account must have **Owner** or **Full User** access to the Search Console property to sync data.
