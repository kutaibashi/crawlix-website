## What AI Bot Detection Shows

Crawlix groups the 12 tracked AI crawlers by company, showing you which AI companies currently have access to your content based on your `robots.txt` configuration.

Note that some companies use multiple bots. For example, Anthropic uses both `anthropic-ai` and `ClaudeBot`. Blocking one but not the other means the company still has partial access.

## Why This Matters for Publishers

Understanding which AI companies can crawl your content is important for:

- **AI training data decisions** — some publishers don't want their content used to train AI models
- **AI-generated answer monitoring** — knowing which AI services may cite your content
- **Licensing and attribution** — some AI companies have licensing programs for content publishers

## Access Status

For each company, Crawlix shows:

- **Fully allowed** — all the company's bots are permitted
- **Fully blocked** — all the company's bots are blocked
- **Partially blocked** — some of the company's bots are blocked but others are allowed
