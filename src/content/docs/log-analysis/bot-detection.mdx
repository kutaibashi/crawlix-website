## How Bot Detection Works

Crawlix classifies each log entry by analyzing the user agent string. Every request is assigned to one of these categories:

- **Search engines** — Googlebot, Bingbot, DuckDuckBot, Yandex, Baidu, and other indexing bots
- **AI crawlers** — all 12 tracked AI bots (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, Google-Extended, etc.)
- **SEO tools** — Semrush, Ahrefs, Majestic, Moz, Screaming Frog, and similar
- **Social media fetchers** — Facebook, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, and other social scrapers
- **Monitoring services** — uptime monitors, status page checkers
- **Human users** — requests with browser user agents (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, etc.)
- **Unknown bots** — unrecognized, non-browser user agents

## Bot vs Human Ratio

The summary view shows the overall split between bot and human traffic for the imported log period. A typical site might see 30–70% bot traffic. Unusually high bot ratios can indicate:

- Heavy crawling by SEO tools
- AI crawler activity
- Distributed denial of service (DDoS) or scraping

## Googlebot Crawl Frequency

Crawlix shows how often Googlebot crawled your site during the log period:

- Pages crawled per day (on average and by day)
- Most frequently crawled pages
- Crawl budget distribution — are bots spending time on your most important pages?

## AI Crawler Volume

See which AI crawlers have been visiting your site and how frequently:

- Total requests per AI crawler
- Most frequently crawled pages by AI bots
- Timeline of AI crawler activity

This is useful for publishers who want to understand whether AI companies are indexing their content heavily.

## Unknown Bots

User agents that Crawlix doesn't recognize are grouped under "Unknown Bots." Review this list to identify scrapers, new bots, or misconfigured user agents.
